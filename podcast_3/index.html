Wake Up Your Creativity

Podcast with Phil Cook

Podcast: "Wake Up Your Creativity – 4 Simple Keys"
(Guest: Phil Cook)

Host

Hello everyone, and welcome to our show! I'm so happy you're here with us today. We have a very special guest – Phil Cook. Phil is not only a musician and songwriter, but also one of the most creative people I've ever met. I think you're really going to enjoy this conversation. Welcome, Phil!

Phil

Hey! Thanks so much for having me. I'm really excited to talk about creativity with you and your listeners.

Host

Phil, I love the way you talk about creativity. You say creativity is like "breathing out." That's such an interesting idea. Could you tell us what you mean by that?

Phil

We breathe in all day – news, social media, stress, noise… everything comes in. Creativity is the moment we finally breathe out. It's letting what's inside you come out. It feels so good. Sometimes, after a long day, I sit down with my guitar or a notebook and I just let my ideas flow. That's my way of breathing out.

Host

That's a beautiful way to think about it, Phil. I think a lot of our listeners can relate to that feeling. You know, so many people, including myself sometimes, say "I'm not creative at all!" What would you say to someone who feels that way?

Phil

That's not true. Look at your normal day:
- What song do you turn up loud in the car?
- What food do you order because you love exactly how it tastes?
- What color did you choose for your room or your clothes?
These little choices = you ARE creative. You just didn't call it creativity yet.
Even small decisions show your unique taste and style. I think creativity lives in those everyday moments.

Host

I love that answer. It really makes me see things differently. So, you have 4 simple rules to help people wake up their creativity. Let's talk about them! What's the first one?

Phil

Rule 1: Drop the heavy expectations.
Stop thinking you have to be Picasso or get a million likes. Just be you. That's enough.
When I write music, I try not to worry if it's "good enough." I just enjoy the process.

Host

I think that's such important advice. It's so easy to put too much pressure on ourselves. Okay, what's Rule 2?

Phil

Rule 2: Lower the stakes – make it less serious.
Example: you paint your nails bright yellow, then suddenly think "What will people say?"
Tell yourself: "It's only nail polish! No big deal!"
The more scared you feel, the more personal (and good) it probably is.
Taking risks, even small ones, can help you discover new parts of yourself.

Host

That reminds me of when I tried wearing bright green shoes for the first time! I felt nervous, but it was also really fun. Now, what about Rule 3?

Phil

Rule 3: Bring yourself to the work.
I'm clumsy, so I always look at the ground when I walk. One day I noticed I only pick up rocks with stripes. Now my whole house is full of striped rocks!
My biggest problem (being clumsy) became my favorite creative thing.
It's funny how our little habits and quirks can lead to something special.

Host

That's wonderful, Phil. I love how you turn something small about yourself into something creative and positive. Okay, are you ready to share Rule 4?

Phil

Rule 4: Find your safe place – your sanctuary.
It can be:
- your car when your favorite song plays
- the shower
- the garden
- or just a small rock in your pocket
When you touch it, you remember: "I'm still me. Everything is okay."
Having a safe place helps you relax and open your mind to new ideas. Sometimes, I find inspiration in the quietest moments.

Host

That's such good advice, especially for people who feel nervous or unsure. Phil, I know you also make something really special and beautiful with your hands… what are they called?

Phil

I call them "worry sticks."
I take a piece of wood and slowly carve it with a knife while I think about someone I love. At the end there's a little place for your thumb to rub when you feel worried.
I never sell them. I only make them for very special people. I made one for you today.

A worry stick crafted with love

Host

(emotional) Phil… I'm actually holding it now. It's so soft and smooth. It really feels like your hand. Thank you so, so much for this thoughtful gift.
Sometimes, a small gift like this means more than words can say.

Phil

Keep it in your pocket. When you feel stressed, just rub it.
It's a tiny reminder that someone cares about you.

Host

That's really touching, Phil. Thank you. Before we finish, do you have any last words or advice for our listeners?

Phil

Remember: you belong to yourself. Nobody else is inside your body. Nobody else has your voice.
Talk to yourself like your best friend would.
And today, do one tiny thing you always wanted to do:
wear funny socks, sing loud, draw something…
Do it today!
Even if it feels silly, these small acts make life brighter.

Host

Phil Cook, you just taught all of us how to breathe again. I love you, and I'm so grateful for your time and your kindness today.

Phil

Love you too. Thank you for this space. I hope everyone listening feels a little more creative and a little more free.

Host

To everyone listening: friends, take a deep breath right now… and breathe out.
Go show a little piece of yourself to the world today.
I promise your life will feel brighter.
Thank you for joining us. See you in the next episode – take care!
